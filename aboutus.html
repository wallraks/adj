Welcome to ADJ

What we Offer

  • Protect DJs from unfair legislation
  • Host events without interference and financial liabilities that creat losses. Seek waivers where applicable, or benefits from organizations that require payments from DJs
  • Register and unite all DJs
  • Have the support of artistes, and support artistes as DJs e.g. DJs buy music at significant discounts or receive music free
  • Set minimum earnings for DJs: 5,000/- net income from individuals and small organizations, and 50,000/- net income from corporates and other large organizations
  • Streamline standards of DJ education. Have a common syllabus and examination board, plus all students must invest in their own headphones, cartridges and slipmats, and learn on turntables and vinyl before proceeding to technology and software.
  • Help all DJs acquire their own equipment within 3 months of graduation
  • Streamline protocol of professionalism to be recognized as true DJs and how to interact with clients
  • Outline minimum requirements and working conditions for clients to procure DJs
  • Creative ways of boosting the economy and the GDP through music
  • Backing from international bodies, foundations, NGOs, embassies, governments of other countries, and other influential DJs worldwide
  • Regular meetings on industry education and fellowship, such as every 3 months
  • Help DJs get new music as it comes out, especially gospel music and gospel music videos
  • Encourage and promote a buying culture in society
  • DJs become active in the war on piracy in partnership with artistes
  • DJs support CSR activities and projects that build society positively
  • Use technology to disseminate gospel music and gospel music videos to the public
  • Performing Rights Society of Kenya
  • Kenya Association of Music Producers
  • Public Relations Society of Kenya
  • Promoting local music internationally and international music locally

The Aim

  • To elevate the livelihood of DJs
  • To have the industry and the public recognize & appreciate DJs
  • To make the music industry a significant contributor to the national GDP
  • To make the music industry and its DJs a significant tourism export
